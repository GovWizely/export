China – Nuclear

30 Sept 2015
Share This
by John

The Chinese government is actively promoting nuclear energy as a clean, efficient and reliable source of electricity generation. It is on pace to triple its nuclear capacity to 58 GWe by 2020 and 150 GWe by 2030. In this regard, China has 23 operational nuclear power reactors, 26 under construction. China plans to ramp up its construction of reactors in the coming years and build an estimated 10 reactors per year after 2020.

China also plans begin marketing its indigenously designed reactors for export in 2015.

As China has becomes increasingly self-sufficient for its nuclear power technology needs, imports of nuclear power related equipment will decline. However, ample opportunities exist for U.S. industry in new builds and all aspects of the fuel cycle. Nuclear fuel assemblies are fabricated and supplied indigenously and the country’s energy policy states that PWRs will be the primary but not sole reactor type, and that domestic manufacturing of plants and equipment will be maximized.

Nuclear currently constitutes less than only 2% of installed electricity capacity in China but is projected to constitute 7% by 2040. According to the China Nuclear Energy Association (CNEA), investment in China’s nuclear industry reached $9.2 billion in 2014, 6.6% lower than in 2013.

Source: International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA); World Nuclear Association

Much of this growth in power generation will come not only from the construction of new nuclear sites, but also from the development of more advanced plant designs. Whereas most plants currently in operation have a production capacity of 2 GW, the vast majority of reactors currently under construction are using more advanced designs. The designs most favored in current construction plans are the CPR-1000 and American designed AP1000 generators, which have a production capacity of around 1.25 GW. In the case of the AP1000, this imported design is forming the main basis of China’s move towards 3rd generation nuclear technology.
